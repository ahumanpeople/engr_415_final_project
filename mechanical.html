Mechanical Design

The mechanical design is centered on a classic wide-brimmed black fedora, fully modeled in SolidWorks. The goal was to conceal every electronic component while keeping them accessible for maintenance and iteration.

Three VL53L5CX-SATEL Time-of-Flight sensors are mounted along the brim — two at the front for coverage of the user's primary field of motion and one at the rear — providing a near-360° sensing arc. Eight ERM haptic vibration motors are secured just above the brim's inner edge, each corresponding to a directional zone.

The STM32 microcontroller and LiPo battery are housed in a laser-cut basswood enclosure mounted on top of the hat's crown. Basswood was selected for its ease of laser-cutting at small scales and adequate thermal dissipation for outdoor use. 3D-printed PLA spacers create the necessary clearance to sandwich the battery and MCU board within the enclosure.
