Electrical Design

The system is built around an STM32L476RG Nucleo board, selected for its faster ARM Cortex-M4 architecture over Arduino — necessary to simultaneously interpret 16+ data points from multiple ToF sensors and drive 8 independent haptic motor channels in real time.

Each VL53L5CX-SATEL sensor communicates over I²C, sharing the bus with address-select lines to differentiate between units. Motor control is handled via 8 GPIO pins configured for PWM output, each driving an S8050 NPN transistor that amplifies the MCU's logic-level current to the motor. A 1N4148 flyback diode in parallel with every motor protects against reverse-voltage spikes.

Power is supplied by a 3.7 V / 3000 mAh LiPo cell boosted to 5 V by an MT3608 DC-DC converter. FSR pressure sensors on the brim's inner edge trigger hardware interrupts to wake or sleep the system — a 1 kΩ pull-up resistor prevents signal jitter at each FSR node.
